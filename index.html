On your own

Pick a schema & stick with it!

Schema is just a fancy word for how your database is set up (table names, field names, unique keys, domains).

Don't start anything until you've picked your schema and level of detail.

But be willing to adjust your schema over time (changing needs, lessons learned).

Decision: Sidewalk as street tag vs. separate way?

Separate way benefits:

  • Map breaks w/o splitting street
  • Indicate segment issues
  • Crossings clear

Separate way detriments:

  • Need to add street name for directions (managing street name in 2 places)

Analysis

Download

If your data is in OpenStreetMap, you'll need a local copy for analysis.

QGIS

You could use many programs, but QGIS has all the tools you need in an easy graphic interface.

QuickOSM

The QuickOSM plugin is an easy way to extract data based on a query.

Once the data is in QGIS, you can filter based on attributes such as "crossing" = 'unmarked'

Or symbolize to highlight areas of interest (like unmarked crossings)

QNEAT3

QNEAT3 is a plugin with most of the tools you'll need.

Shortest Path

Go to QNEAT3, Routing, Shortest Path. Select your from and to points.

Shortest Path

Make a copy of your data & play around w/ adding crosswalks to see how this affects your shortest path.

Routing

APIs like OpenRouteService use OpenStreetMap data.

Isochrones

Isochrones are lines connecting points of equal distance/time.

Isochrones

For pedestrian modeling, we figure out how fast most folks walk and use that distance. A 20 minute mile is normal.

Isochrones

In QNEAT3, we use Iso-Areas. Here is Iso-Area as Polygon from Point.

Isochrones

And here is Iso-Area as Polygon from a layer (in this case a 10-minute walk from parks).

You could run isochrones from any point layer of interest. Calculate a 10 or 20 minutes walk from:

  • schools
  • grocery stores
  • transit stops

Use your results to inform discussions about zoning, bus routes, e-scooters, public amenities & services.

  • Abrupt sidewalk ends
  • Lack of crosswalks
  • No lowered kerb
